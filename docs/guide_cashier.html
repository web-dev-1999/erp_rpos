Introduction & Login

Your Role as Cashier

The Cashier is responsible for the financial close of every table transaction. Your core duties are:

  • Process all payments (cash, card, online/digital wallet, mixed methods)
  • Apply approved discounts and vouchers to bills
  • Split bills between guests when requested
  • Issue printed or digital receipts
  • Escalate voiding requests to a Manager
  • Reconcile the cash drawer at end of shift
ⓘ
Access Level

The Cashier role includes everything the Waiter role can do (view tables, send orders to kitchen) plus the ability to open the Payment Screen, confirm payments, and apply discounts. Voids and system-level overrides require a Manager.

Accessing the POS

  1. Open a web browser on the POS terminal.
  2. Navigate to http://[server]/desk/rpos — replace [server] with the IP or hostname given by your IT team (e.g. 192.168.1.10 or pos.restaurant.local).
  3. On the ERPNext login page, enter your username (usually your work e-mail) and password.
  4. Click Log In. The POS Floor Plan screen opens automatically.

3

Single-Method Payment — Cash

Use this flow when the entire bill is paid in cash.

  1. Open the Payment Screen via the 💳 Pay button.
  2. Verify the Grand Total on the left panel matches the amount the guest expects to pay.
  3. If the guest pays the exact amount: skip to step 5 (no amount needs to be entered; the system will use the Grand Total as the tendered amount).
  4. If the guest pays a different amount (e.g. hands over PKR 3,000): use the numpad to type the tendered amount — e.g. 3000. The display updates as you type.
  5. Tap the Cash button. The system logs this as a cash payment for the entered (or exact) amount.
  6. The Change Due field turns green and displays the amount to return to the guest (e.g. PKR 680).
  7. Hand the change to the guest.
  8. Tap Confirm Payment. The table is cleared and moves back to Available (grey).

⚠
Underpayment

If the tendered amount is less than the Grand Total and you tap Confirm Payment, the system will alert you. You cannot confirm until the full amount is covered. Either collect the remaining cash or switch to a mixed-payment approach (see Section 5).

4

Single-Method Payment — Card

Use this flow when the entire bill is settled on a single credit or debit card. The POS does not integrate directly with card terminals — it records the method for reporting purposes; the physical card swipe/tap is handled by your card terminal device separately.

  1. Open the Payment Screen via the 💳 Pay button.
  2. Process the payment on the physical card terminal first: swipe/insert/tap the card and confirm approval.
  3. Once the card terminal shows Approved, return to the POS screen.
  4. Do not enter any amount on the numpad — the full Grand Total is charged automatically.
  5. Tap Credit Card or Debit Card as appropriate. The full Grand Total is applied.
  6. No change is applicable for card payments.
  7. Tap Confirm Payment. The table clears.

5

Mixed Payments

Mixed payments allow a guest to pay part of the bill in cash and the rest on card (or any combination of methods). The POS tracks each portion separately.

Example Scenario

Grand Total: PKR 2,320. Guest wants to pay PKR 1,000 cash and the rest on credit card.

  1. Open the Payment Screen.
  2. Using the numpad, enter 1000 (the cash portion).
  3. Tap Cash. The system records PKR 1,000 as cash and the Balance Remaining updates to PKR 1,320.
  4. Process PKR 1,320 on the physical card terminal and confirm approval.
  5. Back in the POS, the remaining balance (PKR 1,320) is pre-filled. You may also type it manually on the numpad to confirm.
  6. Tap Credit Card. The balance drops to PKR 0.
  7. Tap Confirm Payment. The table clears.
📷
Screenshot Placeholder

General Rules for Mixed Payments

  • You can combine any number of methods — tap each method button after entering its portion on the numpad.
  • The Balance Remaining always shows how much is still outstanding.
  • Each method is logged as a separate line in the payment record for reporting purposes.
  • You cannot confirm the payment until the Balance Remaining is exactly PKR 0.
  • To remove a partially entered method, tap Clear on the numpad and re-enter the correct amount.
✅
Three-Way Split

You can apply three or more methods by repeating the enter-amount → tap-method cycle as many times as needed. For example: cash + credit card + online wallet.

6

Online / Digital Wallet Payments

The Online payment method covers digital wallets, mobile payment apps, bank transfers, and QR-code payments (e.g. JazzCash, EasyPaisa, bank mobile app transfer).

  1. Ask the guest to complete the payment on their phone or digital wallet app to the restaurant's registered account/QR code.
  2. Verify the transaction notification or screenshot from the guest (or check the restaurant's wallet app for the incoming transfer).
  3. On the POS Payment Screen, enter the amount received on the numpad (or leave blank for the full amount).
  4. Tap Online. The system records this as a digital payment.
  5. Optional — add a reference: tap the 📝 Notes field that appears and type the customer's transaction ID, phone number, or any reference provided. This helps with reconciliation.
  6. Tap Confirm Payment once the full amount is covered.

7

Bill Splitting

Bill splitting allows you to divide a table's order into separate bills, each paid independently. This is common when a group wants to pay individually or when two couples share a table but pay separately.

ⓘ
When to Split

Always split the bill before processing any payment. Splitting after a partial payment has been confirmed is not supported and will require manager intervention.

Step-by-Step: Splitting a Bill

  1. From the Order Screen (not the Payment Screen), tap the ✂ Split Bill button in the toolbar. The Split Bill dialog opens.
  2. All items in the order are listed on the left side with their quantities. On the right, you will see bill assignment buttons: Bill A and Bill B.
  3. For each item, tap the bill it belongs to:
    — Tap Bill A to assign the item to Guest A's bill
    — Tap Bill B to assign the item to Guest B's bill
  4. More than 2 guests? Tap + Add Bill to create Bill C, Bill D, etc. Additional assignment buttons appear for each new bill.
  5. Continue assigning all items until every line has a bill assigned. Unassigned items will be highlighted in amber as a warning.
  6. Tap Confirm Split. The system creates separate sub-bills. You are returned to the Order Screen where each bill now appears as a separate tab (Bill A, Bill B, etc.).
  7. Process each bill through the normal payment flow: select a bill tab → tap 💳 Pay → complete the payment → Confirm.
  8. The table clears only after all sub-bills are fully paid.

Handling Shared / Fractional Items

If two guests share a dish (e.g. one pizza split between two people), the system does not support sub-item splitting. The recommended approach:

  • Assign the shared item to the bill of whichever guest will pay for it.
  • The guests settle the fractional cost between themselves — the restaurant collects the full item price from one bill.
  • Alternatively, if both guests want to share the cost equally, ask the waiter to re-enter the item as two half-portions (if the menu supports it).
✅
Tip — Re-splitting

If you need to change the bill assignment after Confirm Split has been tapped, tap ✂ Split Bill again to re-open the dialog and reassign items. No payments must have been started on any bill for this to work.

Applying Discounts

Discounts can be applied to an entire bill before payment is processed. There are two types: a fixed PKR amount deducted or a percentage reduction on the total.

How to Apply a Discount

  1. From the Order Screen, tap the % Discount button in the toolbar. The Discount dialog opens.
  2. Choose the discount type:
    — Fixed Amount: deducts an exact PKR value from the bill total (e.g. PKR 200 off)
    — Percentage: deducts a percentage of the subtotal (e.g. 10% off)
  3. Enter the discount value using the on-screen keyboard or numpad.
  4. Tap Apply. A Discount line appears in the bill summary, showing the deducted amount. The Grand Total updates immediately.
  5. Proceed to payment as normal.

Printing Receipts

Printing at Payment Time

  1. On the Payment Screen, locate the Print Receipt toggle near the Confirm Payment button.
  2. Ensure the toggle is switched ON (green). If you do not want a printed receipt, leave it OFF.
  3. Tap Confirm Payment. The receipt is sent to the thermal printer automatically upon payment confirmation.
📷
Screenshot Placeholder

Thermal Printer Requirements

  • The thermal printer must be powered on, loaded with receipt paper, and connected to the network or the POS terminal.
  • Printer configuration (IP address, port, paper width) is set by your IT administrator. Contact IT if the receipt does not print.
  • If the printer is offline, the payment will still complete — the receipt will not print. You can reprint later (see below).

Re-printing a Receipt

If a receipt failed to print or a guest requests a duplicate:

  1. Open ERPNext desk: navigate to Accounts > POS Invoice.
  2. Search for the invoice by table number, date, or amount.
  3. Open the invoice and click 🖶 Print in the top toolbar.
  4. Select the POS Invoice print format and click Print / Send to Printer.
ⓘ
Digital Receipt

If your restaurant has email receipts configured, you can also e-mail the invoice directly from the POS Invoice record. Open the invoice → click the ✉ Email button → enter the guest's address.

Handling Voids (Escalation to Manager)

🚫
Cashiers Cannot Void Sent Items

Once an item has been sent to the kitchen (status: Sent or Prepared), it cannot be removed from the bill by a Cashier. This is a deliberate control to prevent revenue leakage. A Manager must authorise all voids.

What is a Void?

A void removes an item from the current order and notifies the kitchen to stop preparation (if not already done). The item is struck from the bill and will not be charged to the guest.

How to Request a Void

  1. Identify the item the guest wishes to remove on the Order Screen.
  2. Tap the item to select it, then tap the 🚫 Void Item button.
  3. A Manager Authorisation dialog appears. The cashier cannot proceed past this dialog.
  4. Call your manager to the POS. The manager enters their username and password in the dialog, then selects a void reason from the dropdown (e.g. "Guest changed mind", "Wrong item sent", "Complaint").
  5. Manager taps Confirm Void. The item is removed from the bill and a void notification is sent to the Kitchen Display System (KDS).
  6. The updated bill (without the voided item) is reflected immediately. If payment was in progress, the Grand Total is recalculated.

⚠
After a Payment is Confirmed

Voids cannot be performed after a payment is confirmed. If a guest disputes a charge after payment, the manager must raise a Refund / Credit Note through ERPNext Accounts. This is outside the scope of the POS cashier workflow.

End-of-Day Checklist

At the end of your shift, complete the following steps in order to ensure the POS is correctly closed and all transactions are reconciled.

✍ End-of-Day Checklist — Cashier

  • Confirm all tables are settled — no amber (bill-requested) tables remain on the Floor Plan.
  • Check that no table is stuck in a partial-payment state (Balance Remaining > 0).
  • Count the physical cash in the cash drawer.
  • Compare cash drawer total with the POS Cash Summary for your shift (ask manager to run the POS Closing Entry report).
  • Record any discrepancy (over or short) on the shift discrepancy log and inform your manager immediately.
  • Confirm all discount applications are accompanied by valid authorisation notes.
  • Confirm all split bills have been fully settled (no open sub-bills).
  • Verify the receipt roll in the thermal printer is sufficient for the next shift; replace if low.
  • Log out of the POS: tap your name/avatar in the top-right corner → Log Out.
ⓘ
POS Closing Entry

Your manager will close the POS session in ERPNext (POS Closing Entry), which generates the official daily sales report. You do not need to create this entry yourself, but you may be asked to confirm the closing cash amount.

Common Cashier Scenarios

The following Q&A covers the most frequent edge-case situations you will encounter at the payment counter.

A guest realises they have a voucher or discount code after the payment screen has already been opened.
Tap Cancel on the Payment Screen to return to the Order Screen without any changes. Then tap % Discount, apply the voucher value as a Fixed Amount discount (or the voucher's percentage), and re-open the Payment Screen. The discount will be reflected in the updated Grand Total. If the discount exceeds your cashier limit, call the manager before reopening the payment screen.
A guest wants completely separate receipts for each person at the table but did not ask for this when ordering.
Cancel out of the Payment Screen (if open). From the Order Screen, tap ✂ Split Bill and assign items to each guest's bill. Once you tap Confirm Split, each bill appears as a separate tab. Process each tab through the full payment flow independently. Each confirmed payment generates its own invoice and receipt. Ensure the Print Receipt toggle is ON for each payment if physical receipts are needed.
The card terminal shows "Declined" — the guest now wants to pay cash instead.
Do not tap any button on the POS after a card decline. The POS has not recorded any payment yet. Simply inform the guest politely. Now proceed normally: use the numpad to enter the cash amount tendered and tap Cash instead of the card button. The original Grand Total remains unchanged. No manager intervention is needed for this switch.
The guest accidentally overpays (e.g. hands over a large note and you enter the wrong amount).
Before tapping Confirm Payment: tap Clear on the numpad and re-enter the correct tendered amount. The Change Due will update automatically. If you have already confirmed the payment with an incorrect overpayment recorded, inform your manager — a correction must be made through ERPNext Accounts as a refund entry. Never issue change exceeding the actual cash handed to you without first re-verifying the amount.
The guest claims they paid but the table is still showing as Amber (not cleared).
This can happen if the browser lost connection at the moment of confirmation. Check the POS Invoice list (ERPNext → Accounts → POS Invoice) for a recent invoice for this table. If an invoice exists and is submitted, the payment was recorded — tap Refresh on the Floor Plan; the table should clear. If no invoice exists, the payment was not completed; process it again normally. If uncertain, call the manager before accepting any further payments for this table.
A guest is paying with both a digital wallet and cash but the online transfer amount is pending (not yet received).
Do not record the online portion until you have verified it in the restaurant's wallet app. Collect and record the cash portion first (enter cash amount → tap Cash). Then wait for online transfer confirmation. Once confirmed, enter the remaining amount → tap Online (add the reference ID in Notes) → tap Confirm Payment. If the guest is impatient, call your manager to supervise. Never confirm a payment for an unverified digital transfer.
A group of 6 wants to split the bill evenly — how do I divide equally?
The POS does not automatically divide amounts equally. You need to manually assign items to each bill (Bill A through Bill F for 6 guests). For items shared equally, assign each shared item to one guest's bill (they can sort it among themselves), or better — ask the waiter to enter each guest's items separately during ordering to avoid this situation at payment. Equal-split automation is a planned feature; for now, use the manual assignment method.
