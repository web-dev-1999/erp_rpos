Introduction

The Manager role is the highest-privilege level in DineOS. It includes everything a Waiter or Cashier can do — opening tables, taking orders, processing payments, splitting bills — plus elevated actions such as voiding sent items, applying discounts beyond the standard threshold, editing the floor layout, managing reservations, and pulling reports.

Accessing the POS

Open any browser and navigate to:

http://[server]/desk/rpos

Log in with your manager credentials (username and password assigned in ERPNext). The interface will reflect your elevated permissions immediately on login.

🔒
Your credentials are personal. Never share your username or password with waiting staff. All voids, discounts, and layout changes are recorded against your account for audit purposes.

Waiter & Cashier Capabilities

As manager you have full access to all standard floor operations. The summary below is for reference; detailed instructions are covered in the Waiter and Cashier user guides.

Covered in detail in the Waiter Guide
  • Viewing the floor plan and table status (available, occupied, reserved)
  • Opening a table, selecting covers, assigning a waiter
  • Browsing the menu by category and adding items to an order
  • Adding modifiers and special instructions to individual items
  • Sending orders to the kitchen (KDS)
  • Adding or removing items before and after sending
  • Transferring a table or merging two tables
Covered in detail in the Cashier Guide
  • Viewing the bill summary and applicable taxes
  • Processing payment (cash, card, mixed)
  • Splitting the bill by amount, by seat, or by items
  • Printing or emailing receipts
  • Closing a table after payment
ℹ
All cashier and waiter actions performed under a manager login are logged against the manager account. If a waiter uses your credentials, traceability is lost. Always log out before handing a terminal to floor staff.

Voiding a Sent Item

Once an order is sent to the kitchen, standard users cannot delete items. A guest may change their mind, an item may be out of stock, or a waiter may have sent the wrong item to the wrong table. In all these cases, a manager must perform a void.

⚠
A void permanently removes the item from the order and creates an immutable audit record. It does not refund a payment that has already been processed. If payment has been taken, a refund must be handled separately.

How to Void an Item

  1. On the floor plan, tap the table with the item to be voided to open the Order Screen.
  2. Locate the item in the order list. Sent, Cooking, and Ready items display a ⛔ (void) button to the right of the item name.
  3. Tap the ⛔ button. The Manager Void dialog opens.
  4. Enter your own manager username and password in the authentication fields. Do not enter the waiter’s credentials.
  5. Enter a void reason in the text field. This is mandatory — the system will not allow submission without a reason. Choose a clear, specific reason (e.g. "Guest changed order after sending" or "Item 86’d").
  6. Tap Void Item. The item is removed from the order immediately.

What Happens After a Void

  • The item disappears from the Order Screen and bill total adjusts automatically.
  • The KDS shows a VOID notification on the chip for that item so kitchen staff are aware.
  • An audit log entry is created in ERPNext with your name, the item, reason, and timestamp.

Applying Discounts

Discounts can be applied to any open order before payment is finalised. Standard cashier staff can apply discounts up to the configured threshold (typically 10%). Only managers can apply discounts above this threshold or apply a fixed-amount discount.

How to Apply a Discount

  1. Open the table’s Order Screen and verify the order is correct before adding any discount.
  2. Tap the % Discount button in the payment/bill area of the Order Screen.
  3. Select the discount type:
    • Percentage — enter a number between 1 and 100 (e.g. 15 for 15% off).
    • Fixed Amount — enter the exact currency amount to deduct (e.g. 10.00).
  4. For discounts above the waiter threshold, a manager authentication prompt appears. Enter your manager credentials.
  5. Optionally enter a reason (recommended for audit trail).
  6. Tap Apply Discount. The bill total recalculates immediately.

Discount Audit

All applied discounts are automatically logged against the order record. You can review them in:

  • ERPNext → Restaurant POS → POS Order — open any order and scroll to the Discounts section.
  • Sales Analytics — filter by discount type to see aggregated discount impact over a date range.
⚠
Discounts cannot be removed once a payment has been processed. Double-check the discount amount before completing the transaction.

Editing the Floor Layout

The floor plan is a live representation of your dining room. You can reposition tables, add new tables, and adjust their properties directly from the POS interface without going into the ERPNext back office.

⚠
Do not edit the floor during active service. Layout changes take effect immediately for all logged-in staff. Moving a table that has an open order can confuse floor staff. Schedule layout edits during quiet periods.

Entering Edit Mode

  1. From the main floor plan view, tap ✎ Edit Floor in the top-right header. The floor switches to edit mode — tables gain a drag handle and the toolbar updates.
  2. All tables are now draggable. Press and hold a table, then drag it to its new position on the grid.

Adding a New Table

  1. In edit mode, tap + Add Table.
  2. A properties panel opens. Fill in:
    • Table Number — a unique identifier (e.g. T-15).
    • Capacity — maximum number of covers.
    • Shape — Square or Round (affects how the table renders on the floor plan).
  3. Tap Add. The new table appears in the top-left corner of the grid.
  4. Drag it to the correct position in the dining room.

Saving or Discarding Changes

  • Tap 💾 Save Layout to commit all changes. Staff on other terminals will see the updated layout within seconds.
  • Tap Cancel to discard all changes made in this edit session and return the layout to its previous state.

Managing Floors (Back Office)

For deeper floor configuration — creating a brand-new floor, changing the grid dimensions, or setting a background colour or image — use the ERPNext back office rather than the live POS interface.

Navigating to POS Floor

  1. Log in to ERPNext at http://[server]/desk using your manager credentials.
  2. Go to ERPNext → Restaurant POS → POS Floor.
  3. You will see a list of all configured floors (e.g. Ground Floor, Terrace, Private Dining).

Creating a New Floor

  1. Click + New in the POS Floor list view.
  2. Fill in the Floor Name (e.g. Rooftop Terrace).
  3. Set the Background Colour or upload a floor plan image.
  4. Set Grid Size to determine how many grid squares the floor plan uses.
  5. Click Save.

Assigning Tables to Floors

Individual tables are managed in the POS Table doctype (Restaurant POS → POS Table). Each table record has a Floor field. Set it to the correct floor name and save.

ℹ
After creating a new floor and assigning tables, refresh the POS on all terminals so the new floor appears in the floor switcher at the top of the POS screen.

Reservation Management

The Reservations module gives you full control over bookings — creating, modifying, cancelling, and seating guests. Access it directly from the POS header.

Viewing All Reservations

From the main POS screen, tap 📅 Reservations in the header. A list view opens showing all upcoming reservations sorted by date and time, with status indicators (Confirmed, Seated, Cancelled, No-Show).

Creating a New Reservation

  1. In the Reservations view, tap + New Reservation.
  2. Fill in the booking details:
    • Guest Name — full name of the primary contact.
    • Phone Number — for confirmation and reminder messages.
    • Covers — number of guests expected.
    • Table — select from available tables for that time slot.
    • Date & Time — reservation date and arrival time.
    • Source — how the booking was made (Walk-in, Phone, Online, Third-party).
  3. Tap Save Reservation. The table is immediately marked as Reserved on the floor plan (shown in blue).

Cancelling a Reservation

  1. Find the reservation in the list view or by tapping the reserved table on the floor plan.
  2. Tap Cancel Reservation.
  3. A confirmation prompt appears — tap Confirm Cancel. The table status reverts to Available.

Seating a Reserved Guest

  1. When the guest arrives, tap the reserved table (blue) on the floor plan.
  2. Tap Seat Guest. The table switches from Reserved to Occupied and an order session opens.

Auto-Release Rules

  • The system alerts you after 60 minutes of no-show (no guest seated for a confirmed reservation).
  • After 90 minutes of no-show the reservation is automatically cancelled and the table released.
  • Both events are logged in the reservation record for reporting purposes.

Reports & Analytics

All financial and operational reporting is handled through the standard ERPNext reporting suite. Access these reports via the ERPNext desk (http://[server]/desk) using your manager credentials.

Report Location in ERPNext Purpose
Sales Analytics Accounts → Sales Analytics Daily, weekly, and monthly revenue by item, category, table, or waiter
Stock Ledger Stock → Stock Ledger Item-level stock movement; identify consumption patterns and shrinkage
Profit & Loss Statement Accounts → Profit and Loss Statement Full P&L for any period; compare against budget
Customer Acquisition CRM → Customer Acquisition Reservation source breakdown; track online vs. walk-in vs. phone bookings
Void Log Restaurant POS → Void Log All void events with reason, manager, time, and item detail
Discount Report Restaurant POS → Discount Report All discounts applied, by amount and percentage, grouped by date or staff
📌
Tip: Filter all reports by Company "Admin (Demo)" for clean demo results that exclude test transactions. Switch to your live company name for production reporting.

User & Access Management

Staff access is controlled through ERPNext’s standard user management system. Navigate to ERPNext → Settings → User to manage all system users.

Available Roles

POS Manager
  • All POS actions
  • Void items
  • High-value discounts
  • Floor editing
  • Reports access
  • User management
POS Cashier
  • All waiter actions
  • Process payments
  • Split bills
  • Standard discounts (threshold)
  • Print receipts
Kitchen Staff
  • KDS view only
  • Advance chip status
  • No order creation
  • No payment access

Adding a New Staff Member

  1. Go to Settings → User → + New. Fill in the staff member’s full name, email address, and a temporary password.
  2. In the Roles table, add the appropriate role (POS Manager, POS Cashier, or Kitchen Staff). Save.
  3. Go to Human Resources → Employee → + New. Fill in the employee record and link it to the User created above using the User ID field. Save.
  4. The staff member can now log in. Ask them to change their password on first login.

Deactivating a User

Open the User record and tick Disabled, then save. The user will be logged out immediately and unable to log back in. Their historical records are preserved.

⚠
Never delete a User record for a departed staff member. Deleting a user can orphan historical records and break audit trails. Always disable rather than delete.

Common Manager Scenarios

The situations below are the most frequently escalated issues during service. Review them to be prepared.

A waiter accidentally sent the wrong items for two different tables
Step 1 — Notify the kitchen immediately so they can hold preparation on any items not yet started.

Step 2 — Void the incorrect items on both tables using the Manager Void dialog (Section 3). Use reason "Sent to wrong table".

Step 3 — Re-send the correct items from each table’s Order Screen. The kitchen will receive fresh tickets with updated table numbers.

Step 4 — Check with the kitchen whether any voided items are already being cooked. If so, those items may still be served with a waiter’s manual instruction, or discarded — your call based on food safety and waste policy.
End-of-day cash reconciliation
  1. Confirm all tables are settled — no amber tiles remain on the Floor Plan.
  2. From the POS, tap 🔒 Close Session in the top-right header. See Section 11 — Session Management for full steps.
  3. Enter the actual closing cash count in the dialog. The system displays the expected amount (opening float + cash collected).
  4. Confirm the session. ERPNext creates a POS Closing Entry recording any over/short variance.
  5. Deposit surplus cash in the safe and set the opening float for the next shift.
Adding a new seasonal item to the menu
  1. Go to ERPNext → Restaurant POS → POS Menu Item → + New.
  2. Enter the item name, description, price, and food category.
  3. Assign the KDS Station (e.g. Grill, Desserts) so it routes to the correct kitchen column.
  4. Upload an image (optional but recommended for visual menus).
  5. Set Available to Yes and save.
  6. The item appears on the POS menu immediately. Refresh POS terminals if it does not appear within 60 seconds.
Closing a floor for a private event
  1. Ensure all open orders on the floor are closed and paid before making changes.
  2. Go to ERPNext → Restaurant POS → POS Floor and open the relevant floor record.
  3. Set the Status field to Closed and add a note in the Closure Reason field (e.g. "Private event 18:00–23:00").
  4. Save. The floor will no longer appear in the POS floor selector for standard staff.
  5. To reopen, return to the same record and set Status back to Active.

Session Management

Every working shift on the POS must be bounded by an Open Session and a Close Session. Sessions tie all POS Invoices to a single POS Opening Entry in ERPNext, making shift-level cash reconciliation and sales reporting accurate.

Opening a Session

When a cashier or manager logs into the POS and no active session exists, the Open POS Session dialog appears automatically. The user enters the opening cash float (the amount placed in the cash drawer) and clicks Open Session. The POS is inaccessible until this step is completed.

ℹ
Each POS Profile / terminal has its own session. If you have two POS terminals, each must open and close its session independently.

Monitoring Active Sessions (Back Office)

  1. In the ERPNext desk, go to Point of Sale → POS Opening Entry.
  2. Filter by Status = Open to see all active sessions across all terminals.
  3. If a session is stale (left open from a previous day), you can manually set its status to Closed from the Opening Entry record.

Closing a Session

  1. Ensure all tables are settled.
  2. Click the 🔒 Close Session button in the POS top-right header.
  3. Review the shift summary:
    • Session opened — start time of the session
    • Invoices — count of invoices processed this session
    • Total sales — gross revenue for the session
    • Expected cash in drawer — opening float + all cash payments received
  4. Count the physical cash and enter the total in Actual Closing Cash Count.
  5. Click Close Session. ERPNext creates a POS Closing Entry recording the difference between expected and actual cash (variance).

Reviewing Closing Entries (Back Office)

  1. Go to Point of Sale → POS Closing Entry.
  2. Open any entry to view the full payment reconciliation by mode (cash, card, etc.) and the cash variance.
  3. Run the POS Daily Sales Summary report for a revenue breakdown filtered by session.
⚠
Cash variance policy

A positive variance (more cash than expected) should be documented and deposited. A negative variance (less cash) must be investigated before the cashier leaves the premises. Any variance exceeding your restaurant's threshold (set by management) requires a signed discrepancy form.
