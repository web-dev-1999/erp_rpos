1

Introduction & Login

The DineOS is a browser-based point-of-sale system built on ERPNext 17. It is designed to be used on tablets, touchscreens, and desktop workstations and requires no software installation — everything runs directly in the web browser. As a waiter or server, this system is your primary tool for managing tables, placing orders, communicating with the kitchen, and processing payments.

Accessing the System

Open any modern web browser (Chrome or Edge are recommended) and navigate to:

http://[server]/desk/rpos

Replace [server] with the IP address or hostname of your restaurant's server — your manager will provide this. On a dedicated POS terminal or tablet, a shortcut or bookmarked link should already be configured. If the address does not load, check that the device is connected to the restaurant's Wi-Fi or network.

Logging In

The system uses your ERPNext credentials — the same username and password you were assigned when your account was created. Enter your username (usually your staff email address or a short username) and your password, then tap or click Log In.

🔒
Keep your login private. Never share your password with other staff members. Each login is tied to your name, so all orders and transactions processed under your account are recorded against you. If you forget your password, ask a manager to reset it.

After Logging In

Once authenticated, the system takes you directly to the Floor Plan — a live, colour-coded map of all tables in the restaurant. This is your home screen for the duration of your shift. You do not need to navigate through menus or dashboards; every task starts and ends here.

ℹ
Session timeout. For security, the system will automatically log you out after a period of inactivity. If you return to the terminal and find yourself on the login page, simply log in again — any open orders will be preserved.

Understanding the Floor Plan

The Floor Plan is the central hub of the POS. It gives you an at-a-glance overview of every table's status, how long guests have been seated, and how much revenue is building at each table. Everything is updated in real time, meaning all terminals in the restaurant display the same live information simultaneously.

Each table tile on the floor plan is colour-coded to indicate its current status at a glance:

✓

Available — ready to seat guests
●
Amber
Occupied — guests seated, order in progress
📅
Blue
Reserved — booking confirmed for a future time
✹
Purple
Cleaning — table cleared, being prepared

Information Displayed on Each Table Tile

Each coloured tile shows up to four pieces of information, depending on the table's status:

  • Table number — displayed prominently in the centre or top of the tile (e.g., "T12").
  • Seat capacity — shown with a 👤 (person) icon, indicating the maximum number of covers the table can accommodate (e.g., "👤 4").
  • Elapsed time — for occupied tables, a timer shows how long the current party has been seated (e.g., "42 min"). When this timer reaches or exceeds 60 minutes, it turns red as a prompt to check on the table or encourage turnover.
  • Running total — the current value of the open order at that table, updated every time an item is added (e.g., "£47.50").
⏱
Red timer is a nudge, not an alarm. A red elapsed-time indicator simply means the table has been occupied for over an hour. Use your judgement — it may be a leisurely dinner party or a special occasion. The intent is to help you prioritise table checks during a busy service.

Stats Bar

At the top of the Floor Plan screen, a horizontal stats bar displays a live count of tables in each status category: how many are Available, Occupied, Reserved, and Cleaning. This gives you an instant sense of how busy the floor is without counting individual tiles. Managers can use this view to plan staffing; as a waiter, it helps you know how many free tables remain when greeting walk-in guests.

Multiple Floors and Areas

If your restaurant has more than one dining area — such as a Main Dining Room, a Terrace, or a Private Dining room — each area is shown as a separate tab at the top of the Floor Plan. Tap the tab labelled with the area name to switch between floors. Each tab maintains its own colour-coded grid. You can switch between areas at any time without losing any open orders.

Opening a Table

When guests are seated at a table, your first action is to open that table in the POS. This creates an active order linked to that table and starts the elapsed-time timer.

How to Open a Table

  1. From the Floor Plan, locate the table where your guests are seated. It should be displayed in green (Available).
  2. Tap or click the green table tile once.
  3. The Order Screen opens automatically for that table.
  4. On the Floor Plan, the table tile immediately changes from green to amber (Occupied), visible to all terminals.
📷
SCREENSHOT: Order screen that opens after tapping a green table tile
Replace this box with a screenshot of the order screen showing the left item catalogue panel and right running-order panel
⚠
Table already amber? If a table you expect to be free is showing amber, it has already been opened — possibly by a colleague who seated guests moments before you. Tap it to view the existing order rather than trying to open a new one. Never attempt to open a table that is occupied by another party.

Once the Order Screen is open, you are ready to take the guests' order. The table will remain amber until payment is completed, at which point the system automatically resets it to green (or purple if it needs cleaning, depending on your restaurant's configuration).

Assigning a Customer (Optional)

For loyalty members, hotel guests, or corporate accounts you can optionally link the order to a named customer record before taking the order. Walk-in guests need no assignment.

  1. In the order header, tap 👤 Guest (next to Covers and Note).
  2. Search by name or account number and select the customer.
  3. Tap Assign. A blue chip confirms the link.
  4. To remove it, tap the ✕ on the blue chip.
ⓘ
Anonymous orders — if no customer is assigned the system silently uses the default Walk-in Guest account at payment time. You never need to worry about this for regular tables.

Adding Items to an Order

The Order Screen is divided into two main panels. The left panel is the item catalogue — a browsable, filterable list of everything on your menu. The right panel is the running order — the list of items you have added for this table, along with a live subtotal.

📷
SCREENSHOT: Order screen with left catalogue panel and right running-order panel
Replace this box with a screenshot of the split-panel order screen, clearly showing both panels

Using Category Pills to Filter the Menu

At the top of the left panel, a row of category pills (e.g., Starters, Mains, Beverages, Desserts) allows you to filter the catalogue to a specific course. Tap a pill to show only items in that category. Tap the All pill or tap the active category again to clear the filter and see the full menu. A search bar may also be available — type a few letters of an item name to find it quickly.

Adding an Item

  1. Tap the category pill for the course you want (e.g., Starters).
  2. Browse the item cards. Each card shows the item name, a brief description if available, and its price.
  3. Tap an item card to add it to the order.
  4. If the item has modifiers or options, a Modifier Pop-Up appears (see below).
  5. If there are no modifiers, the item is added to the right panel immediately.

The Modifier Pop-Up

Many menu items have customisation options. When you tap an item that has modifiers configured, a pop-up window appears before the item is added to the order. This pop-up may contain:

  • Required choices — marked with a red label (e.g., "Cooking preference: Rare / Medium / Well Done"). You must select one before you can confirm. The item cannot be added without completing a required choice.
  • Optional extras — shown with a price modifier (e.g., "+ £1.50 Extra Cheese"). These are optional; tap to toggle them on or off.
  • Quantity — a numeric field allowing you to set how many of this item to add in one step (default is 1).
  • Special notes — a free-text field where you can type any specific instruction from the guest (e.g., "nut allergy — please check sauce", "no onions"). Notes appear on the kitchen ticket.
⚠
Always record allergy notes. If a guest mentions a food allergy or intolerance, enter it clearly in the Special Notes field. Do not rely on verbal communication with the kitchen alone. These notes are printed on the kitchen ticket and logged against the order.

Once you have made all selections in the modifier pop-up, tap Add to Order (or Confirm). The item appears in the right panel with its chosen modifiers listed beneath it.

Item Status: Pending

All items added to the right panel before being sent to the kitchen are marked with an ⏳ Pending indicator. This means the kitchen has not yet received these items. You can freely edit, remove, or add to the order while items are in the Pending state. Nothing is committed until you tap Send to Kitchen.

Editing Before Sending

Before you tap Send to Kitchen, you have full flexibility to make changes to the order. Use this time to confirm the order back to the guests and catch any mistakes or missed items. Once the order is sent, editing requires manager involvement, so it is worth taking a moment to double-check.

Adjusting Quantity

Each item in the running order (right panel) has a + button and a − button next to the quantity. Tap + to increase the quantity by one; tap − to decrease it. Reducing to zero is equivalent to removing the item.

Removing an Item

To remove an item entirely from the pending order, tap the 🗑 bin (trash) icon on the item row. The item will be removed immediately. You will not be asked for confirmation — if you accidentally remove an item, simply tap it again in the left catalogue panel to re-add it.

Updating the Cover Count

The cover count records how many guests are seated at the table. This is used for per-cover reporting and management statistics. To update it, tap the 👥 Covers button (usually shown with a person icon and the current count) and enter the correct number of guests. This does not affect pricing but is important for accurate management reporting.

Adding an Order Note

To add a general note that applies to the entire order rather than a specific item — for example, "guests celebrating a birthday" or "one guest has a severe nut allergy" — tap the 📝 Order Note button. A text input appears; type your note and confirm. The note will be visible to kitchen staff on the printed docket and in the kitchen display.

Sending to Kitchen

Sending the order to the kitchen is the moment the items leave your control and enter the kitchen's workflow. Until this point, all changes are local to your screen. Once sent, the kitchen immediately receives the order ticket on their Kitchen Display System (KDS) or printer.

How to Send

  1. Review all items in the right panel and confirm they are correct.
  2. Tap the prominent orange Send to Kitchen button at the bottom of the right panel.
  3. All items marked as ⏳ Pending are sent simultaneously in a single ticket.
  4. Each item's status changes from ⏳ Pending to 📨 Sent.
  5. The kitchen sees the ticket immediately on their display.
📷
SCREENSHOT: Orange "Send to Kitchen" button at the bottom of the order panel
Replace this box with a screenshot highlighting the Send to Kitchen button

After Sending

Once items are sent, their status indicator in the right panel changes to 📨 Sent. The items remain visible in your order panel so you can track their progress and know what is outstanding. You can still add new items to the order after sending — new additions will appear as ⏳ Pending until you send again.

🚫
Sent items cannot be deleted without manager approval. Once an item has been sent to the kitchen, it has been received and may already be in preparation. Removing or voiding it at this point requires a manager to enter their authorisation. This is a deliberate control to prevent waste and fraudulent voids. If a guest changes their mind after an item is sent, speak to your manager immediately.

Tracking Order Status

Every item on a sent order moves through a defined status pipeline. You can see the current status of every item on the Order Screen at all times. This allows you to manage guest expectations, time your table visits efficiently, and know exactly when to carry dishes from the pass to the table.

The Status Pipeline

⏳
Pending
→
📨
Sent
→
🔥
Cooking
→
✅
Ready
→
🍴
Served
  • ⏳ Pending — Item added to the order but not yet sent to the kitchen. Only exists on your screen.
  • 📨 Sent — Ticket delivered to the kitchen. Kitchen has received and acknowledged the order.
  • 🔥 Cooking — Kitchen staff have begun preparing the item and have updated its status on the KDS.
  • ✅ Ready — Item is prepared and ready at the pass for collection. A green Mark Served button appears next to the item in your panel.
  • 🍴 Served — You have confirmed delivery by tapping Mark Served. The item is complete.

Marking Items as Served

When an item reaches the ✅ Ready status, a green Mark Served button appears beside it in your order panel. After you have physically carried the dish to the table and placed it in front of the guest, return to the terminal (or use a handheld device if available) and tap Mark Served. This updates the order record and clears the item from the kitchen display's "ready" queue.

💡
Check the order screen between courses. Glancing at the order screen between trips to the floor keeps you informed of what is cooking and what is ready, helping you avoid the kitchen calling for a runner with no one available.

Adding More Items (Mid-Order)

Guests frequently add items after the initial order has been sent — an extra side dish, a second round of drinks, or a dessert. The system is designed to handle this cleanly and efficiently, ensuring the kitchen only receives new items rather than a repeat of the full order.

How to Add Items to an Existing Order

  1. From the Floor Plan, tap the amber (Occupied) table tile for the table in question.
  2. The existing Order Screen opens, displaying all previously sent items with their current status.
  3. Use the left catalogue panel to find and add the new items, exactly as you would when taking an initial order.
  4. New items appear as ⏳ Pending alongside the already-sent items.
  5. Tap Send to Kitchen. Only the new Pending items are transmitted — the kitchen receives a separate ticket for the additions.
⏱
No duplication. The system is intelligent about what has already been sent. When you tap Send to Kitchen with both sent items and new pending items in the order, only the pending items go to the kitchen. Previously sent items are not resent, regardless of how many times you tap the button.

The running total on the order screen (and on the floor plan tile) updates immediately to include the new items, giving you and your manager an up-to-date view of the table's spend at all times.

Right-Click / Long-Press Menu

The Floor Plan offers a context menu for each table tile that gives you access to actions beyond simply opening the order. This menu is designed to let you manage table statuses quickly without navigating away from the floor plan.

How to Access the Context Menu

  • On a desktop or mouse-driven touchscreen: Right-click on any table tile to immediately open the context menu.
  • On a tablet or touchscreen without a mouse: Press and hold (long-press) the table tile for approximately 0.6 seconds until the context menu appears. Do not lift your finger too quickly — a brief tap will open the order instead of the menu.

Context Menu Options

The available options vary slightly depending on the current status of the table, but typically include:

  • Open Order — Opens the Order Screen for that table. This is equivalent to a single tap on an occupied table.
  • Add Reservation — Marks the table as Reserved (blue) and allows you to enter the guest's name, party size, and arrival time. Use this when a reservation needs to be logged during service for a table that is currently available.
  • Mark Cleaning — Changes the table status to Cleaning (purple). Use this immediately after a party departs and before the table has been cleared and reset. This prevents colleagues from accidentally seating new guests at an uncleared table.
  • Mark Available — Returns a table from Cleaning or Reserved status back to Available (green), signalling that the table is ready to accept new guests. Only use this once the table has been fully cleaned and reset.
📷

⚠

Payment

When guests are ready to pay, the payment process is handled entirely within the POS. The system calculates the total, applies any applicable taxes, calculates change, and provides a clear record of the transaction.

Opening the Payment Screen

  1. Open the Order Screen for the table (tap the amber tile on the floor plan).
  2. Tap the 💳 Pay button in the order panel.
  3. The Payment Screen opens, displaying a full itemised bill with individual item prices, any applicable service charge, and the total including tax.
📷
SCREENSHOT: Payment screen showing itemised bill, tax breakdown, and payment method buttons
Replace this box with a screenshot of the full payment screen

Processing a Single-Method Payment

  1. Review the total with the guest.
  2. Enter the amount tendered (for cash) in the amount field, or leave it at the total for card payments.
  3. Tap the appropriate payment method button: Cash, Credit Card, Debit Card, or Online.
  4. For cash payments, the system automatically calculates and displays the change due in green.
  5. Toggle Print Receipt on or off depending on the guest's preference.
  6. Tap ✓ Confirm Payment to finalise the transaction.

Upon confirmation, the order is closed, the receipt prints (if enabled), and the table on the floor plan resets to Available (green) — or to Cleaning (purple) if your venue uses that workflow.

Processing a Mixed Payment (Two Methods)

Guests sometimes wish to pay partly in cash and partly by card (for example, using up gift vouchers, or splitting a personal and business portion). The POS handles this in a straightforward sequence:

  1. On the Payment Screen, enter the cash amount the guest is paying in cash into the amount field.
  2. Tap Cash. The cash amount is recorded and the remaining outstanding balance is displayed.
  3. Enter the card amount (or leave it at the remaining balance) in the amount field.
  4. Tap Credit Card or Debit Card as appropriate.
  5. The total tendered now matches or exceeds the bill total. Tap ✓ Confirm Payment.
ℹ
Multiple payment methods are unlimited. You can add as many separate payment amounts and methods as needed before confirming. The Confirm Payment button only becomes active once the total tendered equals or exceeds the bill total.

Bill Splitting

Guests at the same table may wish to pay separately — either by dividing the bill evenly or by assigning specific items to specific people. The Split Bill feature allows you to allocate each item on the order to a separate bill, then settle each one independently.

How to Split a Bill

  1. From the Order Screen, tap the ✂ Split Bill button.
  2. The Split Bill screen opens, showing all ordered items in a central panel with two default bills: Bill A and Bill B.
  3. For each item, tap the bill label you wish to assign it to (Bill A, Bill B, etc.). The item moves to that bill's column.
  4. If the party has more than two separate bills, tap + Add Bill to add additional bill columns (Bill C, Bill D, and so on).
  5. Continue assigning all items until every item is allocated to a bill.
  6. Tap Confirm Split to finalise the allocation.
  7. You are returned to a modified payment screen where each bill (Bill A, Bill B, etc.) is listed separately with its own total.
  8. Process payment for Bill A using the normal payment process, then repeat for Bill B, and so on.
📷
SCREENSHOT: Split Bill screen showing items being allocated to Bill A and Bill B with the "+ Add Bill" button visible
Replace this box with a screenshot of the bill splitting interface
💡
Tip for large groups. For parties where everyone wants to pay equally rather than by item, it is often faster to process a single payment for the full amount and then manually collect equal contributions from the guests — splitting by item is most useful when individuals ordered distinct items and want to pay only for what they had.
ℹ
Shared items (e.g., a bottle of wine for the table). Items that are genuinely shared can be assigned to one bill, with the understanding that that guest pays for it and is reimbursed by others. Alternatively, if a shared item needs to be split financially, speak to your manager, who can adjust the price on individual bills.

Once all sub-bills are settled, the table closes and resets to Available exactly as it would after a single payment.

Common Scenarios

The following Q&A scenarios address the situations you are most likely to encounter during a shift. Keep this section handy as a reference when something unexpected arises.

⁉ A guest wants to add items after the initial order has already been sent to the kitchen

This is a completely normal and supported workflow. There is no need to close and reopen the order.

  1. From the Floor Plan, tap the amber table tile to open the existing Order Screen.
  2. Add the new items from the catalogue panel in exactly the same way as the initial order.
  3. New items appear as ⏳ Pending alongside the already-sent items.
  4. Tap Send to Kitchen. Only the new pending items are transmitted — the kitchen receives a supplementary ticket clearly labelled as an addition to the existing order.

The table's running total updates automatically to include the new items.

⁉ A wrong item was sent to the kitchen and the guest does not want it

Once an item has been sent, it cannot be removed without manager authorisation. Here is what to do:

  1. Immediately alert your manager and explain the situation.
  2. The manager will log in with their credentials and authorise a void on the specific item.
  3. If the kitchen has already started preparing the item, the manager will also communicate with the kitchen to stop preparation (where possible).
  4. Once voided, the item is removed from the order and the total adjusts accordingly.

To avoid this situation, always read the order back to the guest before tapping Send to Kitchen.

⁉ Guests have left and the table needs cleaning before it can be reseated

Use the context menu to update the table status without affecting any system records:

  1. On the Floor Plan, right-click (desktop) or long-press (tablet) the now-vacated table tile.
  2. Select Mark Cleaning from the context menu. The tile turns purple.
  3. The table is now clearly flagged as unavailable while it is being cleared and reset.
  4. Once the table is fully clean and set, right-click / long-press again and select Mark Available. The tile returns to green.
⁉ A guest wants to pay using two different payment methods (e.g., part cash, part card)

The POS supports mixed-method payments natively:

  1. Tap 💳 Pay to open the Payment Screen.
  2. Enter the cash amount in the amount field and tap Cash. The outstanding balance updates to show the remainder.
  3. Enter the remaining amount in the amount field and tap the appropriate card type (Credit Card or Debit Card).
  4. Confirm the total tendered covers the bill, then tap ✓ Confirm Payment.

The receipt will itemise both payment methods for the guest's record.

⁉ A reserved guest arrives and you need to seat them at a blue table

Reserved tables (blue tiles) are pre-allocated to incoming guests. When the reserved party arrives:

  1. On the Floor Plan, right-click / long-press the blue reserved table tile.
  2. Select Open Order from the context menu to open the order for that table.
  3. The table transitions from Reserved (blue) to Occupied (amber) as soon as the order screen opens.
  4. Proceed to take the guests' order normally.

If a reserved guest does not arrive and the reservation needs to be cancelled, speak to your manager, who can clear the reservation and return the table to Available.
